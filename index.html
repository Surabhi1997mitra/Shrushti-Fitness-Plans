Lifestyle flags — address these first
3 things outside the gym
slowing your results.
1 — Sleep: 5–6 hours is not enough
This is the biggest hidden blocker. Poor sleep increases cortisol, worsens insulin sensitivity, drives cravings (especially for sugar and carbs), and makes fat loss significantly harder — regardless of how clean your diet is. Target: 7–8 hours. Even getting to 7 hours consistently will make a noticeable difference within 2 weeks.
You cannot out-diet poor sleep. It will slow your results more than any single food choice.
2 — Water: 1–2L is too low
At 69 kg with 4 gym sessions a week, minimum 2.5–3L daily. Dehydration causes false hunger signals, reduces gym performance, and slows metabolism. The simplest habit to fix immediately.
Start with a 1L bottle at your desk. Finish it by lunch. Refill. Finish it by 6pm. Add a final glass at night. That's 2.5L with zero effort.
3 — Stress: High
High chronic stress keeps cortisol elevated, which promotes fat storage around the belly and makes weight loss slower and harder. The workout plan and protein targets will help. Adding 10 minutes of any wind-down activity before bed (reading, stretching, breathing) goes a long way.
Alcohol on weekends also raises cortisol and disrupts sleep. Even 1–2 drinks can knock out 2 days of recovery. It doesn't have to be eliminated but keep it to 1 drink, not 2–3.

Light, crunchy, and very filling. Dry roast with black salt and jeera. Low calorie, low GI, very easy to snack on without overeating.
~90 kcal · Low fat · Low GI · Easy desk snack
6. Banana — 1 medium
Best consumed pre-workout. Avoid as a random snack — too much sugar without protein to balance it. Always pair with protein (curd, egg, or whey) if eating it outside workout context.
~90 kcal · Fast carbs · Best used pre-gym
7. Cucumber — unlimited
Slice and eat with black salt and lemon. Zero guilt, very hydrating. Kills hunger without adding anything to the calorie count. Always keep some in your PG fridge.
~15 kcal per cup · 97% water · Zero sugar
8. Green tea — 1–2 cups/day
Drink instead of snacking when the craving is more boredom than actual hunger. Slightly appetite-suppressing, zero calories. Avoid adding sugar or honey.
~0 kcal · Light metabolism support · No sugar
Hunger on a deficit is normal — especially in the first 2 weeks. Drink 1 full glass of water first and wait 10–15 minutes. If still hungry, eat from the list above. Cucumber is unlimited.

Progressive Overload — Ascending Pyramid Format
For most machine and isolation exercises tagged Pyramid below, we follow an ascending pyramid — weight goes up while reps come down, across 3 sets.

· Set 1: lighter weight × 15 reps
· Set 2: middle weight × 12 reps
· Set 3: heaviest comfortable weight × 10 reps
Example: if 35kg is your heaviest comfortable weight on a machine — do 25kg × 15, 30kg × 12, 35kg × 10. As your heaviest weight goes up over the weeks, the whole pyramid shifts up with it. Wherever you see the Pyramid tag, this is the format — no need to re-read this every time.

Training notes — beginner reminders
Form before weight
Week 1 is about learning the movement patterns. Lighter weight, more reps, focus on feeling the right muscle working. Add weight from week 2 onwards once form is dialled in.
Never train fasted
Always have the pre-workout banana + coffee before the session. Training without fuel (especially at a calorie deficit) burns muscle, not just fat.
Hydration during gym
500ml–750ml water during the session. Dehydration kills performance and makes sessions feel unnecessarily hard.
Sleep is training
Recovery happens during sleep — this is when muscle is built and fat is mobilised. 5–6 hours is not enough. Getting to 7 hours will measurably improve how you feel and how fast your body changes.
Soreness vs pain
Muscle soreness (especially 24–48 hours after a session) is normal as a beginner. Sharp pain during a movement is not — stop and report it. Soreness reduces within 2–3 weeks as the body adapts.
Blood reports — recommended
No current blood reports on file. Recommend a basic panel (CBC, Vitamin D, iron, thyroid) at the 6-week mark to check for any deficiencies that could be slowing progress.
6 days is intense — listen to your body
This is a demanding schedule for a beginner. If a session feels off — low energy, joint discomfort, persistent fatigue — it's okay to swap that day for a walk and resume the next day. Sunday is your only full rest day, protect it.

Progress milestones
What to expect and when
Week 1–2
Mostly water weight + bloating reduction. Scale may drop 1–2 kg. Energy might feel lower as the body adapts to deficit — this is normal.
Week 3–4
Actual fat loss begins. Expect 0.5–1 kg loss per week if adherence is good. Gym sessions should start feeling more familiar.
Month 2
Body composition starts visibly changing — not just scale weight. Clothes fit differently. Strength in gym should be going up consistently.
Month 3
Target review at 90 days. Blood reports recommended. Calorie and workout plan to be adjusted based on new weight and progress.
